Elijah Watson, MA

I am a sixth-year PhD candidate in Anthropology and Master of Public Health (MPH) student in Epidemiology at Northwestern University, graduating in June 2026. I am an NIH F31 Fellow funded by the National Institute on Aging and previously held a National Science Foundation Graduate Research Fellowship (GRFP).

My research integrates biomarkers of aging—especially epigenetic clocks—with causal inference methods and ecosocial theory to understand how social and environmental conditions become embodied across the life course.

Dissertation Research

Drawing on the Cebu Longitudinal Health and Nutrition Survey (CLHNS) and Demographic and Health Surveys (DHS) in the Philippines, my dissertation examines how political-economic, environmental, and policy shocks shape human development and health:

  • 1983 Philippine Crisis: Analyzes how prenatal exposure to the 1983 political–economic crisis influenced growth, development, and biological aging through midlife.
  • Typhoon Odette (2021): Investigates how housing damage affected water insecurity, mental health, and epigenetic aging measured one year later.
  • Education Reform and IPV: Uses national DHS data to test whether a policy that made secondary schooling free reduced women’s lifetime risk of intimate partner violence.

MPH in Epidemiology Research

  • COVID-19 Exposure Inequities in Chicago: Examined how neighborhood-level racial and economic segregation mapped onto disparities in COVID-19 exposure prior to vaccine rollout, using Bayesian multilevel regression with poststratification to address sampling and test uncertainty.
